Who is better at problem solving?

Potential questions that I had thought of for my maths cba 2:

1)-What is the most effective chess opening?

2)how many people play chess in my school?

How I arrived at my final questions:

For my CBA 2, I knew I wanted to do something related to chess. So I researched about the most effective chess openings. After careful consideration and guidance from my teachers and friends, I realised that this topic did not meet my requirements for my CBA 2 as it did not provide me with primary data. This is when I started looking at other subtopics related to chess. Therefore, I arrived at my final topic: chess vs. non-chess players; who is better at problem-solving?

Variables And Constants

Variables:

Independent variables

1) Chess experience: Chess players and Non- chess players

Dependent variables

1) Problem solving ability

2) Time taken to solve the problems

3) Accuracy to solutions

Constants

1) Types of puzzles given : should be same to ensure fairness

2) Puzzle difficulty: similar difficulty to ensure consistency

3) Same testing conditions

4)Same instructions given: Providing the same instrustctions to all the participants

Assumptions

1) Chess Skill Development: chess players develop specific problem-solving skills that differ from non-players.

2) Understanding Puzzles: All participants have a similar comprehension of the logic puzzles and instructions.

3)Comparable Cognitive Abilities: Participants within the same age group have similar cognitive skills.

4)Equal Motivation: All participants are equally motivated to perform well on the puzzles.

5)Environmental Consistency: The testing environment does not significantly influence participant performance.

6)Puzzle Appropriateness: The complexity of the puzzles is suitable for the participants age and cognitive levels.
